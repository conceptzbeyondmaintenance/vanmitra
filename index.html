OUR IMPACT

Within just one year of inception, Van Mitra has demonstrated the power of regenerative agriculture and community-driven action. From transforming long-barren land to empowering tribal women, every initiative contributes to rebuilding ecosystems and livelihoods while promoting sustainability and biodiversity.

The Challenges

  • The land had remained barren for decades, as locals believed it was unsuitable for farming.
  • Lack of livelihood opportunities among native tribal communities in the region.
  • Low biodiversity and environmental neglect due to prolonged non-agricultural use of land.

Our Solutions

  • Revive barren land through organic soil restoration techniques like vermicomposting and biogas utilization.
  • Plant 500,000 Moringa trees to expand green cover and rejuvenate the ecosystem.

Results So Far

In just one year, we’ve grown over 400 kg of Moong, produced nutritious fodder, and planted 40,000 Moringa trees ready for harvest. Our 1,000 Papaya trees now yield about 2,000 kg of Papaya monthly, and 200 Passion Fruit plants are nearing harvest. These efforts have revived biodiversity, attracting birds, butterflies, and small wildlife to previously barren land.

Why Weʼre Unique

Van Mitra blends agroforestry, organic innovation, and community empowerment to build a self-sustaining ecosystem powered by local resources. Our model promotes environmental health, rural employment, and ecological resilience, restoring harmony between people, productivity, and the planet.
